02 Research Interests

Where biology
becomes a threat vector.

SNP
Genomic Variant Detection & Data Integrity

Automating SNP analysis using NCBI reference sequences and peak pattern recognition — and ensuring the reliability, traceability, and security of variant outputs used in diagnostic decision-making.

QMS
Quality Systems for Genomic Pipelines

Applying ISO 9001/13485 frameworks to sequencing workflows — CAPA, SOP development, non-conformance investigation — as a lens for understanding where genomic pipelines are vulnerable.

✂︎
CRISPR & Gene Editing Security

The biosecurity implications of accessible CRISPR-Cas9 toolkits — from dual-use research concerns to the integrity of sequencing data used to confirm successful edits.

0x
Bioinformatics Pipeline Vulnerabilities

Security of open-source genomic tools, supply chain risks in clinical sequencing software, and how data flows through diagnostic pipelines without adequate access controls.

03 Projects

Things I've built
and questioned.

Genomics

Automated SNP Variant Detector

Co-developed a SNP analysis model using NCBI reference sequences and peak pattern recognition to automate variant detection, reducing errors by ~10–15%. Designed for diagnostic traceability.

ISO 9001 · QMS

ISO-Aligned QMS for SNP Genotyping

Implemented a simulated ISO 9001-aligned continuous improvement QMS for an SNP genotyping workflow. Conducted CAPA, non-conformance investigations, and developed audit-ready SOPs — improving accuracy by ~15%.

CRISPR · Wet Lab

CRISPR-Cas9 ADE2 Mutation in Yeast

Performed CRISPR-Cas9 to mutate ADE2 in yeast, confirming successful mutation introduction using sequencing data. Executed full Gibson Assembly and plasmid design workflows.

Research · Cambridge

CRISPR & Diabetes — Linacre Institute

Led a team researching how CRISPR-Cas9 could address diabetes as a global health burden. Presented at the University of Cambridge — won 2nd place. Also presented a poster on Tibetan high-altitude gene-environment interactions.
